My 4 Ride or Die Pens:


Pens are so personal. No one pen is the perfect pen for everyone, we’re all different. But these pens are my favorite, “never gonna give you up”, pens.
  1. The TWSBi Eco- Ergonomically, no, it’s not my favorite. It’s not even the suggested grip. It’s the hard drop off past the suggested grip, towards the nib that bothers me. However, the Eco is such a user friendly pen, you can’t not love it. There’s a reason it’s a top seller.  Easy to clean, works great with even the most stubborn shimmers and pigments (I’m looking at you Pennonia-Strigoi), and a demonstrator to add to the fun.
  2. Kaweco Al-Sport- While I would never, the Kaweco Al-sport is begging to get thrown around and beaten up. Put down the kid gloves, these are tough little pens. Unlike the regular Sport, the Al-Sport has a heft to it that makes it feel significant in your hands. Yes, sometimes you can get a less-than desirable nib, but I’ve had mostly great experiences.
  3. Walltown Catsburg- A maker pen! Working with Walltown is such a delight and the pens Brett produces are some of the most stunning I’ve seen. These pens feel special in your hand, like they’re made with love.
  4. Pilot Custom 823- Yes, pricey. Yes, vac-fillers can be a pain. But oh the buttery nib. Chef’s kiss. Pilot knows how to make a glass-like nib, and the 823 has a huge #15 nib. Cleaning this pen is tiring, and if you’re OCD about leaving moisture in the barrel, you’re best to stay far away. But that’s ok. More for the rest of us.
